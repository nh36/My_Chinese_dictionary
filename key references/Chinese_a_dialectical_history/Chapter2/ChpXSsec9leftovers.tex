\section{How Xiéshēng series developed : the rebus principle}

The Xiéshēng series in the Chinese script reveals intriguing facets of its development, showcasing both iconic logograms and the application of semantic evolution and the rebus principle.

One foundational logogram in this series is 田 (\textit{{\sil{lˤiŋ}}}), representing the concept of `field.' This iconic character visually captures the essence of agricultural landscapes.

The script's adaptability is evident in the semantic extension of 田. Over time, it evolves into \textit{{\sil{rˤujʔ}}} and later becomes \textbf{畾}, demonstrating the script's capacity for semantic evolution.

Applying the rebus principle, 田 (\textit{{\sil{lˤiŋ}}}) transforms to represent `hunt' and eventually becomes \textbf{畋}. This showcases the script's flexibility in conveying different meanings through sound associations.

Another pivotal logogram in the series represents `fire' - \includegraphics[scale=0.3]{Chapter1/fire.png} (\textit{{\sil{qʷʰˤəjʔ}}}). Initially coded with complexity, it later simplifies to the concise \textbf{火}. The visual representation effectively captures the essence of flames, illustrating the script's ability to depict complex concepts iconically.

The logogram for `fire' also undergoes semantic extension, evolving into \textit{{\sil{tsˤə}}} and later becoming \textbf{災}. This showcases the script's adaptability in expressing nuanced meanings.

An iconic representation of `nose' coded as \includegraphics[width=5mm]{Chapter1/bjiyH.png} (\textit{{\sil{bits}}}) later becomes \textbf{鼻}. This logogram exemplifies the script's ability to visually capture the essence of body parts.

Furthermore, the rebus principle is observed again with \includegraphics[width=5mm]{Chapter1/bjiyH.png} (\textit{{\sil{s.bits}}}), originally depicting `nose,' evolving to represent `self' in the character \textbf{自}. This highlights the script's creative use of sound associations.
